On the experimental side, we're currently building a brand new cryogenic vacuum apparatus for performing experiments with metastable qubits, which offer a flexible platform for achieving high-fidelity quantum gates. We're also working on a scheme to store quantum information in the quantum oscillator modes of trapped ions in order to enable dense packing of data into just a few ions. Both of these projects take advantage of our strong collaboration with the trapped ion team at MIT's Lincoln Laboratory, enabled via MIT's Center for Quantum Engineering. Check out our research page for more details! Theory is often motivated by the intuition and resource constraints of experiment; we continue to look toward applying the techniques or engineering, sensing, or adversarial models to theoretical results to motivate new and useful subfields in quantum information.
